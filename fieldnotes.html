03 / Fieldnotes

EVIDENCE

The practice develops through repeated acts of looking, recording, listening, mapping and testing.

DIGESTION

Territories consume and are consumed.

Trade routes, invasive species, extraction, conflict and river systems are read together as forces that reorganise landscapes across scales.

RECORDING

Decay leaves a readable surface.

Cracks, corrosion, vegetation, flooding marks, sediment, contamination and residual movement become a field vocabulary for understanding what the site is doing.

ENTROPY

Order is never complete.

Industrial control is interrupted by informal access, ecological intrusion, hydrological processes and material failure. Design begins in those interruptions.

MAPPING

Maps are arguments about what matters.

We combine archival plans, walking routes, ecological traces, contested boundaries and future scenarios to make hidden relations spatially legible.

Territorial

Gradient of ruination

Dock, industry, marsh and fort read as one changing system.

Temporal

Future floodscape

2050 and 2100 conditions used to guide phased occupation and retreat.

LISTENING

Sound reveals occupation before form does.

Wind, water, birds, machinery, footsteps and reverberation help identify where the site is active, inaccessible, inhabited or changing.

Ambient

Weather and water

Record cycles, thresholds and intensities that drawings alone cannot hold.

Situated

Voices and routes

Use conversation and walking to understand how people already negotiate the ground.
