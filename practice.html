01 / Practice

PRACTICE

Iribiopolis works with sites that have become unstable: materially, ecologically, politically or socially.

MANIFESTO

Ruination is treated as a condition to work with, not a defect to erase.

01

Read before repair.

We begin with material decay, hydrological traces, ecological intrusion, informal occupation and contested memory.

02

Keep the conflict visible.

Design should not flatten a difficult site into a clean story. It should show where uses, histories and futures remain unresolved.

03

Work with non-human forces.

Tide, sediment, vegetation, weathering and succession are not background conditions. They are design agents.

04

Open rather than complete.

We use cuts, thresholds, routes and partial structures to create access without pretending the site has reached a final state.

05

Build through relations.

Local knowledge, field observation, public conversation, ecological expertise and making shape the proposal together.

NAME

Iribiopolis

A city imagined through remains. The name joins ruin with polis: damaged ground understood not as absence, but as a field of civic, ecological and spatial possibility.

APPROACH

From evidence to spatial proposition.

Recording, mapping, listening, drawing, modelling, public exchange and prototyping are used to move between what a site has been, what it is becoming and what forms of shared life it might support.
